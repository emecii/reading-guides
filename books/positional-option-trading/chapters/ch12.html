Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
Interest Rate Realities
BSM assumes a constant rate, but traders face bid-ask spreads on cash and varying yield curves. Most traders ignore 'rho' unless rates move significantly.
2
Dividend Adjustments
The model is easily adjusted for dividends by subtracting the present value of expected payments from the stock price, keeping the core BSM framework intact.
3
The Shorting Constraint
When a stock is 'hard to borrow,' the BSM replication fails. Traders must adjust the 'cost of carry' to reflect the difficulty of hedging.
4
Stochastic Volatility
While volatility is not constant, using complex stochastic models often adds more noise than precision. Simple adjustments to BSM remain the industry standard.
