Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
Strategy Building Blocks
Directional trading involves five core strategies: Long Call, Call Spread, Short Put, Put Spread, and Risk Reversal. Each has a unique risk-reward profile.
2
The Call Spread Advantage
Call spreads are often superior to long calls because they reduce the cost of entry and mitigate the negative impact of the variance premium.
3
Covered Calls as Multi-Factor Trades
Covered calls capture both equity market risk and the volatility premium. They offer equity-like returns with lower overall volatility.
4
Risk Reversals and Skew
Selling a put to buy a call (risk reversal) is most effective when the volatility skew is steep, allowing the trader to get 'paid' to take a directional bet.
