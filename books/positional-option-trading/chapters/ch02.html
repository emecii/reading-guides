Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Edge Requirement
Discipline and hard work are insufficient without a quantifiable edge. Sinclair posits that a disciplined trader with a bad plan will simply lose money faster.
2
EMH Frameworks
The market is generally efficient, meaning prices reflect available information. However, inefficiencies exist due to structural market constraints and human irrationality.
3
Behavioral Finance Opportunities
Psychological biases like overconfidence and loss aversion create predictable patterns of mispricing. These biases are hard-wired into human nature, ensuring some inefficiencies persist.
4
Alpha Decay and Structural Change
Trading edges are not permanent; they decay as they become crowded or as market structures evolve. Traders must identify the lifespan of their strategies.
