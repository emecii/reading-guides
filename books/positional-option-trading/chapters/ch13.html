Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Rule of Five
By taking just five random measurements, you can be 93% certain the population median lies within that range. This is a powerful tool for quick volatility estimation.
2
The Rule of Three
If an event hasn't happened in N trials, there is a 95% chance the probability of it happening is less than 3/N. This helps estimate 'black swan' risks.
3
Triangular Distributions
When analysts give a range (low, high, most likely), traders can convert this into an annualized volatility input for option pricing.
4
Heuristic Limitations
These rules assume independent observations and constant probabilities. They are 'rules of thumb,' not laws of nature, and must be used with caution.
