Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Inevitability of Forecasting
Every trade is a forecast. Even 'reactive' traders are forecasting that current trends will continue or revert. Successful trading requires making these forecasts explicit.
2
Data Over Intuition
Human intuition is poorly suited for volatility estimation. Sinclair advocates for historical comparisons and data-driven models to avoid the 'narrative fallacy'.
3
The Power of Ensembles
Combining multiple forecasting models (GARCH, time-series, and implied volatility) typically outperforms any single model by smoothing out individual errors.
4
Event-Driven Volatility
Forecasting around specific catalysts like earnings or FOMC meetings provides a more solvable problem than generic time-series forecasting.
