Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
Edges vs. Rules
Sinclair distinguishes between 'rules' (which become obsolete) and 'edges' (general principles based on market phenomena). Successful traders focus on the latter.
2
The Danger of Crowding
When too many traders follow the same strategy, it becomes unstable. Divergence strategies (trend following) are particularly prone to bubbles and crashes when crowded.
3
Term Structure Profits
The shape of the volatility curve (contango vs. backwardation) is a high-confidence signal for selling or buying options.
4
Event Anomalies
Post-earnings announcement drift (PEAD) and the 'overnight effect' represent persistent anomalies where the market misprices information flow.
