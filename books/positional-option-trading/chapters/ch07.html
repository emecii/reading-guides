Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Complexity of Direction
Predicting direction is harder than predicting volatility. In options, you can be right about the direction but still lose money if the move isn't large enough.
2
Subjective Pricing Models
Traders should modify the standard BSM model to include their own 'drift' or expected return. This reveals whether an option is 'cheap' relative to their personal forecast.
3
Skewness of Returns
Options naturally possess positive skewness. This means they offer a small chance of a massive payoff, which must be balanced against the cost of the premium.
4
Strike Choice Metrics
There is no single 'best' strike. Choice depends on whether the trader wants to maximize Sharpe ratio, total profit, or probability of success.
