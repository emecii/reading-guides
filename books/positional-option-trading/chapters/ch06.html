Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Illusion of 'Repair'
Traders often try to 'fix' losing trades. Sinclair argues this is a fallacy; one should only ask if the current position is the best one for the current forecast.
2
Straddles vs. Strangles
Straddles have less downside risk than strangles, despite strangles having a higher win rate. The 'fat tails' of strangles can lead to catastrophic losses.
3
Conservative Spreads
Butterflies and condors limit downside exposure but increase transaction costs. They are 'defined risk' alternatives to naked volatility selling.
4
Skew and Strike Selection
Optimal strike selection involves maximizing the capture of the variance premium, which is often found in OTM puts due to the volatility skew.
