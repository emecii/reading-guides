How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Insurance Demand
The variance premium exists because investors are willing to pay a surplus for protection against market crashes. This creates a systematic gap where implied volatility exceeds realized volatility.
2
Cross-Asset Persistence
This premium is not limited to stocks; it is observed in bonds, currencies, and commodities, suggesting it is a fundamental feature of financial risk management.
3
Jump Risk and the Peso Problem
Some of the premium is 'earned' by sellers who take on 'jump risk'‚Äîthe threat of sudden, catastrophic moves that models cannot predict.
4
Profitability of Short Volatility
Because of this premium, strategies that involve selling options (like shorting VIX futures or index straddles) have a historical tailwind toward profitability.

S&P 500 VIX Gap

Contango in VIX Futures
VIX futures usually trade higher than the spot price, leading to 'roll decay'.

The Insurance Metaphor
