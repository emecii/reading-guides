Argument Flow

How the Chapter Builds Its Case

1
The Goal of Risk Management
Risk management cannot make a bad strategy profitable, but it ensures a good strategy survives long enough to win. It is the foundation of professional trading.
2
The Kelly Criterion
The Kelly Criterion provides the mathematically optimal size for a bet to maximize long-term wealth. However, it often leads to high volatility that most traders cannot stomach.
3
Fractional Kelly
Professional traders often use 'Half-Kelly' or smaller fractions to account for parameter uncertainty and to reduce the risk of catastrophic drawdowns.
4
Non-Normal Outcomes
Option returns are rarely normal. Sizing must account for negative skewness (small wins, huge losses) to avoid 'blowing up' during black swan events.
